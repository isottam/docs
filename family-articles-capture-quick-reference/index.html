Family Articles Capture — Quick Reference

This is the family-wide cheat sheet for getting an article — paywalled or free, on any device — into your Obsidian vault. Pick your device, pick your channel, follow the icon.

For Matthew · Jennie · Isabella · William · Sophie — anyone in the household

The rule is simple: find the article, tap one button, walk away. The vault fills itself up; you read it later in Obsidian.

You don't pick folders. You don't paste text. You don't worry about images or paywalls. You just push the URL into the system — by sharing from an app, by clicking a browser extension, or by speaking the link — and within seconds it's a clean Markdown note in your Knowledge & Ideas vault with images downloaded, byline preserved, and a permalink so you can revisit the original if needed.

Easiest of all — automatic channels

Set-and-forget — zero per-article action required

Channel · FT bookmark sweep
FT

Just tap the FT app's bookmark button — daily sweep handles the rest

This is the easiest FT path full stop. Tap the in-article bookmark / "save for later" icon in the FT app on any device (or click "Save" on ft.com). Once a day at 06:00, a daemon on the household Mac walks the FT saved-articles list and captures every new bookmark into the K&I vault. You don't run a Shortcut, don't open a browser extension, don't think about it. Best-night-out-of-the-week category.

👉 Your gesture

Tap the bookmark icon on any FT article — iPhone FT app, iPad FT app, ft.com in Safari, ft.com in Chrome. All four save into the same FT-side bookmark list.

⚙️ What happens

Daily at 06:00 the ft-bookmark-sweep daemon walks ft.com/myft/saved-articles, captures every new UUID via cli.py, and tracks processed IDs in a state file so bookmarks don't double-capture.

👨 Matthew

His own daemon runs on his Mac.

👩 Jennie

Same — when she taps bookmark in her FT app, it lands in the shared household FT account's bookmark list, which Matthew's daemon sweeps. Zero setup on her Mac.

👧 Isabella / William / Sophie

Standalone-stack users — their own daemon runs on their own Mac against their own FT sub. Same gesture, their own pipeline.

🪶 Cost per article

0 taps. You're already tapping bookmark anyway when you mark something to read later.

Channel · K&I Substack-emit
SS

Subscribe to Substack newsletters via email — daemon emits each into K&I

Forward Substack subscriptions to substacks@isotta.net. The household K&I-substack-emit daemon polls that inbox every 15 minutes and emits each newsletter into the K&I vault as a clean Markdown note. Newsletters file themselves while you sleep. Zero per-article action.

👉 Your gesture

When subscribing to a new Substack publication, use substacks@isotta.net as the email address. Existing subscriptions can be migrated by updating the subscription email on the Substack publication page.

⚙️ What happens

Every 15 min the k-and-i-substack-emit daemon polls Matthew's Inbox/Substack MS Graph folder, parses each newsletter HTML, filters platform noise (no-reply / on / post @substack.com), and emits YYYY-MM-DD SS <Publisher> — <Title>.md into the K&I inbox.

👨 Matthew

His daemon runs on his Mac, processes substacks@isotta.net (his identity).

👩 Jennie

Shared K&I vault — his daemon's output is hers too. No daemon on her Mac. Zero setup.

👧 Isabella

Question for deploy time — does she want her own substacks alias + daemon, or share the household feed? Default is share. If she wants her own, set up substacks-isabella@isotta.net forwarder + install the daemon per isabella-deployment/.

🪶 Cost per article

0 taps. You're already subscribed.

Pick your device — for everything the automatic channels don't cover

What to use on iPhone, iPad, and Mac

Device · iPhone
📱

On the smaller screen — share-sheet first

iPhone reading happens inside publisher apps (FT app, Substack app, Telegraph app) or Safari. The iOS Share Sheet is your one button. Tap Share, find the "Send to Vault" shortcut, and you're done.

🔗 Send to Vault (Shortcut)

Works in any app's share sheet. Posts the URL to your home Mac, which fetches the article and writes it into K&I. Best for FT, Telegraph, Substack.

✂️ Obsidian Web Clipper (Safari)

Tap the WC button in the Safari share row. Best for free articles + Substack notes. Note — paywalled NYT/Bloomberg/Times don't work via iOS WC; share-sheet to Mac instead.

📰 FT app share

Inside the FT app, tap the in-article share icon (NOT Safari's share-sheet — Safari emits a different URL form that FTAdapter can't bypass).

⚠️ Tailscale required

The Shortcut needs Tailscale connected to reach the Mac. If you see "could not find hostname", open Tailscale.app on the iPhone and toggle it.

Device · iPad
📲

The 90% reading surface

iPad is the canonical reading device for this household. Most captures originate here. Use Send to Vault for paywalled content + Web Clipper for free articles.

🔗 Send to Vault (Shortcut)

Same Shortcut as iPhone. Best for Telegraph, FT (via FT app), Substack. Posts to your home Mac.

✂️ Obsidian Web Clipper (Safari)

Works for free articles. Don't use for NYT, Bloomberg, or Times — iPad WC only captures the above-paywall portion. Use Mac Safari WC instead for those.

🔋 Vault open first

Open Obsidian and let it finish indexing BEFORE you clip. WC clips during indexing land as 0-byte files and silently disappear.

🎯 Vault dropdown

After importing templates, check the Vault dropdown in WC settings shows "Knowledge and Ideas" — it can default to the wrong vault on Chromium-based browsers.

Device · Mac
💻

Power user surface — every option works

Mac has the most options. Web Clipper in Safari is the canonical fallback for paywalled NYT, Bloomberg, and Times. ⌘⇧V captures a URL by hotkey. The Send-to-Vault Shortcut is also available.

✂️ Obsidian Web Clipper (Safari + Chrome)

Best for general web pages, Substack, Bloomberg, NYT, Times. Click the WC button → confirm vault → save. Images localised automatically by Local Images Plus.

⌘⇧V Capture to Vault (Shortcut)

Hotkey from any URL bar or selected text. Fastest for Mac-based reading; bypasses share sheets entirely.

🖱 Right-click "Send to Vault"

From any URL, right-click → Services → Send to Vault. Same as ⌘⇧V but discoverable.

🖥 cli.py (terminal)

For batch captures or scripted workflows. python3 ~/claude/scripts/2-capture/articles/cli.py <url>. Defaults to K&I; pre-flight checks publisher cookies.

Publisher · Substack
SS

Best path = subscribe via email

For newsletters you read regularly, subscribe via substacks@isotta.net — the K&I Substack-emit daemon files each new edition into K&I every 15 min, automatically. For ad-hoc reading (notes, posts you found via search, paid posts), use the in-app share or the Shortcut.

⭐ Subscribe with substacks@isotta.net

Best path for newsletters you read repeatedly. Auto-files every edition. Zero per-article action.

✅ Inside the post → Share

Best path for ad-hoc reading. Use the Substack app's in-post Share, not the home feed Share (home-feed URL is slower to resolve).

⚠️ Home feed → Share

Works, but slower (canonical URL resolved server-side via SS-2).

✅ Web Clipper

Works on any device for any Substack URL. Best for Substack Notes (the Shortcut handles those too, but WC is cleaner).

Publisher · NYT / Bloomberg / Times
🔒

Mac Safari Web Clipper ONLY

These three publishers use TLS-fingerprint bot detection that defeats both the Shortcut and iPad WC. The only working path is Mac Safari Web Clipper, where your real Safari session masquerades correctly.

❌ iPhone / iPad anything

Doesn't work. Don't bother trying.

✅ Mac Safari Web Clipper

The canonical fallback. Works because you're already logged in.

🪀 Long-press → Copy Link → AirDrop to Mac

If you spot something on iPhone, AirDrop the URL to your Mac and clip there.

If something doesn't work, try a different device + channel before asking — Mac Safari Web Clipper is the universal fallback for nearly everything.

Each capture path has a sweet spot and a blind spot. The matrix above tells you the sweet spot per publisher. If you hit a blind spot (clip lands empty, no images, paywall fade), switch device or channel and try again. Don't fight it.

Capture cheap once. The hard part — fetching, cleaning, localising images, attaching metadata — happens automatically. Your job is the tap.
